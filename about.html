About the school

A private, co-educational boarding school in Namayumba, built around the idea that education works best when it grows the whole child.

History

Founded in 2010

Extreme High School was officially opened on 7 July 2010 by the Vice President of Uganda. Since then it has grown from a small founding class into a full boarding institution serving students from Senior One through Senior Six.

Because Uganda's school system mirrors the English model, students move through six senior grades on the way to national examinations, with the first class completing Senior Six at Namayumba a few years after opening.

Mission

Growing the whole child

The school's mission centres on the "whole child" — physical, intellectual, emotional, social and spiritual development — delivered by professional caregivers alongside classroom teachers rather than teachers alone.

That shows up as boarding pastoral care, a working farm students help run, a choir that performs beyond Uganda's borders, and an open-door policy toward families who need flexibility to access it.

Academics

Exam-focused curriculum

A curriculum aligned to Uganda's national syllabus and examinations, taught by fully certified staff, from Senior One through Senior Six.

Boarding

Full boarding & day options

Boarding places give students a stable, supervised home during term time; day attendance is also available for local families.

Beyond the classroom

20+ clubs and activities

From the farm to the choir loft, students are expected to take part in at least one club that develops a skill outside exam subjects.
